01 · Astronomical Anchor

Anchored in the sky, not the clock.

Unlike calendars that turn at an arbitrary midnight, the Solar Hijri calendar begins at a physical event: the precise instant the Sun crosses the celestial equator moving northward. That exact crossing gives Nowruz its authority.

The mathematics of spring

At the March equinox, Earth’s axis remains tilted at roughly 23.44°, but the geometry of its orbit places the Sun directly above the equator. The result is a moment of planetary balance: day and night stand in near-equal proportion across the globe. That is why Nowruz feels less like a convention and more like a cosmological threshold.

02 · Ritual Timeline

Nowruz unfolds as a sequence, not a single date.

The Persian New Year is experienced through preparation, purification, gathering, and release. Each ritual transforms domestic space, public space, and the inner emotional space of the household.

Weeks Before Nowruz

Khooneh Tekouni

خانه‌تکانی

Literally “shaking the house,” this ritual is an intensive spring cleaning that clears dust, clutter, and stagnation. It is both practical and symbolic: the household prepares itself to receive the new year in a state of order, dignity, and freshness.

Before the Equinox

Haft-Seen Preparation

هفت‌سین

Families gather objects, polish mirrors, sprout sabzeh, arrange candles, and prepare the symbolic table that will frame the exact turning of the year. The domestic interior becomes an exhibition of memory, fertility, beauty, patience, and hope.

Eve of the Last Wednesday

Chaharshanbe Suri

چهارشنبه‌سوری

Bonfires are lit at dusk and people leap over the flames, exchanging illness and heaviness for energy and warmth. Fire becomes a ceremonial medium of renewal, absorbing exhaustion and returning vitality.

Exact Equinox Moment

Sal-e Tahvil

سال تحویل

Family members gather around the Haft-Seen table and wait for the exact astronomical instant. Greetings, embraces, blessings, sweets, and gifts follow immediately. Time is not estimated; it is witnessed.

13th Day of the New Year

Sizdah Bedar

سیزده‌بدر

The festive cycle closes outdoors. Families picnic in parks and open landscapes, returning the sabzeh to running water and symbolically releasing misfortune. Renewal ends not indoors but in contact with nature.

Ritual logic

Seen as a whole, the sequence moves from cleansing to ignition, from preparation to alignment, and from domestic symbolism to collective presence in nature.

03 · The Haft-Seen

A symbolic table of seven beginnings.

The Haft-Seen is not decoration. It is a structured visual language. Each object beginning with the Persian letter س gathers a distinct value into one ceremonial composition.

Featured Image The Haft-Seen as a complete symbolic composition

The Haft-Seen is most powerful when seen as a whole. Before moving through each individual symbol, this image invites the visitor to encounter the table as a carefully arranged world of growth, sweetness, beauty, patience, protection, memory, and dawn — all gathered at the threshold of a new year.

The Haft-Seen gathers living growth, sweetness, fragrance, patience, and light into a single ceremonial image of the new year.
04 · Sonic Landscape

Music gives Nowruz an emotional architecture.

Persian classical music turns seasonal transition into sound. The timbre of the tar, the breath of the ney, and the phrasing of vocal avaz deepen the reflective atmosphere of the new year.

06 · Closing Reflection

A new year measured by sky, home, table, sound, and season.

This exhibition treats Nowruz as a multi-sensory system: astronomical precision, ritual choreography, symbolic arrangement, musical atmosphere, and culinary memory all converge into one of the world’s most sophisticated seasonal celebrations.

Nowruz is a designed experience.

Its enduring force comes from structure. Geometry appears in architecture, ceremony appears in sequence, meaning appears in objects, and renewal appears in action. This site translates that logic into a digital exhibition with layered motion, scroll-based pattern shifts, interactive symbolic panels, and responsive media layouts.
